Key Project — Cross‑Platform Planning & Simulation Tool

A fully offline, encrypted planning tool I built in Unity for Mac and Android. It shows what happens when you own the whole system — from maps to multiplayer to hardware sensors.

  • Offline maps: Users pick an area and the app saves the map tiles locally. It reads several coordinate formats and lets you switch between them without losing your place.
  • Real‑time planning: Place and control units on the map. Give them waypoints with rules, and move whole groups together using NavMesh pathfinding.
  • Drawing & measuring: Draw directly on the map, add grids, and measure the real distance between any two points.
  • Multiplayer with roles: One device hosts the session. A spectator role can watch everyone's view and take over when needed.
  • Sensor input: A depth sensor scans a physical surface — the app reads the height data and creates matching terrain on the digital map in real time.
  • Security: All sessions are encrypted from start to finish.
  • Stack: Unity 3D · C# · NavMesh · Sensor SDK · Netcode for GameObjects · Newtonsoft.Json · custom geospatial math
